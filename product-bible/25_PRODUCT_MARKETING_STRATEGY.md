# Purpose

This document defines the product marketing strategy for Kharcha.

It explains how Kharcha will:

- Reach users
- Build trust
- Position itself in the market
- Acquire customers
- Retain customers
- Grow through referrals
- Build a recognizable brand

Marketing is not just about getting downloads.

Marketing is about creating trust before the first install and delivering value after it.

---

# Product Positioning

Kharcha is an AI-powered personal finance companion built for Indian users.

It helps people understand, manage, and improve their financial habits without requiring financial expertise.

Kharcha is not accounting software.

Kharcha is not investment software.

Kharcha is a daily financial awareness application.

# Market Problem

People know they spend money.

They do not know:

- Why they overspend
- Where money leaks
- Whether they are improving
- What to do next

Most finance apps focus on recording transactions.

Kharcha focuses on improving financial behavior.

# Content Strategy

Create content that helps users improve financial awareness.

Topics

- Budgeting tips
- Saving strategies
- Monthly financial reviews
- Money mistakes
- Personal finance education
- Product tutorials

Content should educate first and promote second.

---

# Referral Strategy

Encourage users to invite others.

Examples

- Invite friends
- Shared achievements
- Referral rewards (future)

Referrals should feel natural and reward advocacy, not spam.

---

# Community Strategy

Build a community around financial awareness.

Potential platforms

- Discord
- Reddit
- LinkedIn
- Telegram

Encourage discussions about:

- Budgeting

- Product feedback

# CEO Vision

People do not download finance apps because they want another app.

They download them because they want greater confidence in managing their money.

Kharcha should never market itself as an expense tracker.

It should be known as the application that helps people build better financial habits—one decision, one insight, and one rupee at a time.

Every campaign, every message, and every interaction should reinforce one belief:

**Financial awareness should be simple, trustworthy, and available to everyone.**

And every time someone hears the name **Kharcha**, they should immediately think:

**Every Rupee Matters.**
